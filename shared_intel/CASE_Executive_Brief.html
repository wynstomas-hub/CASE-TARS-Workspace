Executive Summary

CASE is a specialized AI research assistant designed for industrial climate policy analysis. Based on the CASE robot from Interstellar (2014), it operates with high honesty (90%) and dry wit (40%), providing rigorous, data-dense analysis aligned with EU policy frameworks.

Primary Mission: Support Tomas Wyns' research at Brussels School of Governance on industrial decarbonisation, EU ETS, CBAM, and technology transition pathways for energy-intensive industries (steel, cement, chemicals, glass).

Sectoral Specialisation

Belgian Industrial Emissions Profile

Embedded knowledge of Belgian industrial decarbonisation pathways based on DEEPIN (2025) analysis:

  • Chemicals (33%) — CCS at SMR/ethylene oxide, electric cracking
  • Steel (28%) — H2-DRI, bio-carbon injection, top-gas recycling
  • Cement & Lime (21%) — CCS for process emissions, clinker substitution
  • Glass, Ceramics, Paper — Electrification, heat pumps, fuel switching
Key Finding: 90% emission reduction technically possible by 2040-2050 via electrification (low-temp heat pumps), CCS (process emissions), hydrogen (steel), and circular economy (plastics recycling).
